Perfect Beef Stew For Canning

Source: https://thesaltymarshmallow.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/beef-stew3.jpg

Ingredients

  • TO BROWN THE MEAT:
  • Few Tablespoons Olive Oil
  • 8
  • Pounds Beef Stew Meat
  • 3
  • teaspoons Salt
  • 2
  • teaspoon Pepper
  • FOR THE STEW:
  • 6
  • Tablespoons Butter
  • 3 1/2
  • Cups Onion Chopped
  • 21
  • Cloves Garlic Minced
  • 3
  • Tablespoon Tomato Paste
  • 4 1/2
  • Cups Good Quality Red Wine
  • 18
  • Cups Beef Stock
  • 6
  • Tablespoons Worcestershire Sauce
  • 1
  • tablespoon Balsamic Vinegar
  • 6
  • Bay Leaves
  • 3
  • teaspoon Granulated Sugar
  • 3
  • teaspoon Dried Thyme
  • 10 1/2
  • Cups Carrots Cut into ½ inch chunks
  • 10 1/2
  • Cups Yellow or Russet Potatoes Cut into ½ inch chunks
  • 5 1/4
  • Cups Cremini Mushrooms Cut in Half

Directions

  1. BROWN THE MEAT:
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large heavy-bottom pot or dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  3. While the oil is heating up, add the beef stew meat to a large mixing bowl and sprinkle over the salt, and pepper and stir to coat.
  4. Working in batches, add part of the meat to the pan and sear, turning with tongs until browned on all sides for about 4-5 minutes per batch. Set all of the meat aside on a clean plate.
  5. FOR THE STEW STOVE TOP:
  6. Add the butter to the pot along with the chopped onion, cook, stirring occasionally for 2-3 minutes until softened.
  7. Add in the garlic and cook, stirring constantly for 30 seconds. Then, stir in the tomato paste and cook for one more minute.
  8. Pour the red wine into the pot and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
  9. Pour in the beef stock, Worcestershire sauce, balsamic vinegar, bay leaves, thyme, and return the seared beef to the pot. Bring to a boil to cook off the alcohol.
  10. Stir in the carrots, potatoes, and mushrooms.
  11. Add to jars leaving 1 inch head room, removing air bubbles. Wipe rims of jars and add lids and finger tighten rims.
  12. Can for 90 minutes at 10 lbs of pressure.
