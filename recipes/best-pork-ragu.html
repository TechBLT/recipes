Best Pork Ragu

Source: https://2sistersrecipes.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/DSC_0779-1024x1024.jpg

Ingredients

  • 2/3
  • cup (86.67 grams) of extra virgin olive oil
  • 3
  • large yellow onions - minced
  • 2
  • large cloves of garlic - minced
  • 5
  • large fresh basil leaves
  • 4
  • cans (28-ounces each) of San Marzano peeled tomatoes
  • 1
  • Tbsp. (17.07 grams) salt
  • 8
  • links of Italian sweet sausages
  • 1.5
  • pounds of pork tenderloin
  • olive oil cooking spray

Directions

  1. Directions To Make Pork Ragu:
  2. In a large saucepot, or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over low heat. Toss in onions, garlic, and fresh basil leaves. Simmer on low heat for 5 to 8 minutes, until the onions turn a golden color.
  3. Next, open each can of tomatoes, and in 4 different batches, add one entire can of peeled tomatoes into a blender. Puree the tomatoes for about 1 minute.
  4. Transfer to the pot with the onions simmering. Repeat until all 4 cans have been pureed and transferred to the pot.
  5. Stir in 1 tablespoon of salt.
  6. Cover with a lid and simmer the sauce on low heat, for one hour, stirring occasionally.
  7. After One Hour of Simmering, UNCOVER.
  8. Continue to simmer on the lowest heat, uncovered.
  9. Now it's Time to Sear the Sausage Links:
  10. In a large non-stick skillet, spray lightly with cooking spray.
  11. Next, on medium heat, place all 8 sausage links and sear them for about 3 minutes per side. While they are cooking in the skillet, take a fork and poke each one to release excess grease from each link.
  12. When sausage links are brown on all sides. Then transfer each one into the ragu sauce that has been simmering beside it.
  13. Allow the sausage links to simmer in the sauce for 30 minutes (uncovered) before searing the pork tenderloin.
  14. After the ragu sauce has been simmering for 1 and a half hours, NOW is the time to sear the pork tenderloin.
  15. Sear the pieces of pork tenderloin in the same skillet, searing each one for one minute on each side. (Do not fry the pieces of pork tenderloin) And transfer each piece to the ragu sauce simmering. Continue until all the pieces have been seared.
  16. Continue to simmer the ragu with all the pork, (uncovered ) for an additional 30 minutes.
  17. Turn off heat, and allow the ragu sauce to rest ( for at least 30 minutes or more) before serving.
  18. The total cooking time for the pork ragu is about two and half hours.
  19. How to Serve Pork Ragu:
  20. When ready to serve. You can either transfer all the meats into a large serving platter and serve them at the table as a side with a salad. And the sauce is served over your favorite pasta. Or you can serve them both in a large pasta bowl.
