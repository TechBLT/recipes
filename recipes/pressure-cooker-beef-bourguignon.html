Pressure Cooker Beef Bourguignon

Source: https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Pressure-Cooker-Boeuf-Bourguignon_EXPS_TOHD24_207902_ChristineMa_1.jpg

Ingredients

  • 3
  • pounds beef stew meat
  • 1-3/4
  • cups dry red wine
  • 3
  • tablespoons olive oil
  • 3
  • tablespoons dried minced onion
  • 2
  • tablespoons dried parsley flakes
  • 1
  • bay leaf
  • 1
  • teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/4
  • teaspoon pepper
  • 8
  • bacon strips, chopped
  • 1
  • pound whole fresh mushrooms, quartered
  • 24
  • pearl onions, peeled (about 2 cups)
  • 2
  • garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/3
  • cup all-purpose flour
  • 1
  • teaspoon salt
  • Hot cooked whole wheat egg noodles, optional

Directions

  1. Marinate the beef
  2. Place the beef in a large shallow dish; add the wine, oil and seasonings. Turn the beef to coat. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  3. Cook the bacon
  4. Select the sauté setting on a 6-quart electric pressure cooker and adjust for medium heat. Add the bacon and cook until crisp, stirring occasionally. Remove with a slotted spoon; drain on paper towels. Discard the drippings, reserving about a tablespoon in the pressure cooker.
  5. Add other ingredients
  6. Next, add the mushrooms and onions to the drippings; cook and stir until tender. Add garlic and cook for a minute longer. Press cancel.
  7. Add the beef
  8. Drain the beef and reserve the marinade. Add beef to pressure cooker. Sprinkle beef with flour and salt; toss to coat. Top with bacon; add reserved marinade.
  9. Pressure-cook on high
  10. Lock the lid and close the pressure-release valve. Adjust to pressure-cook on high for 20 minutes. Let the pressure release naturally for ten minutes; quick-release any remaining pressure.
  11. Simmer until done
  12. Select the sauté setting and adjust for low heat. Simmer, uncovered, until the sauce reaches its desired thickness (about 15 to 20 minutes).
  13. How to Store Instant Pot Beef Bourguignon
  14. Store the bourguignon in airtight containers in the fridge; it will keep for a few days.
  15. Place beef in a large shallow dish; add wine, oil and seasonings. Turn beef to coat. Cover and refrigerate overnight.
  16. Select saute setting on a 6-qt. electric pressure cooker and adjust for medium heat. Add bacon; cook until crisp, stirring occasionally. Remove with a slotted spoon; drain on paper towels. Discard drippings, reserving 1 Tbsp. in pressure cooker.
  17. Add mushrooms and onions to drippings; cook and stir until tender. Add garlic; cook 1 minute longer. Press cancel.
  18. Drain beef, reserving marinade. Add beef to pressure cooker. Sprinkle beef with flour and salt; toss to coat. Top with bacon; add reserved marinade.
  19. Lock lid; close pressure-release valve. Adjust to pressure-cook on high for 20 minutes. Let pressure release naturally for 10 minutes; quick-release any remaining pressure.
  20. Select saute setting and adjust for low heat. Simmer, uncovered, until sauce reaches desired thickness, 15-20 minutes. Remove bay leaf. If desired, serve stew with noodles.
