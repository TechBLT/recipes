Best Red Wine Gravy

Source: https://i0.wp.com/everydaylillie.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/image_35de3054-3fb6-4092-9b0d-8397ed4f0f52.jpg?resize=736%2C949&ssl=1

Ingredients

  • Drippings in pan from one seasoned cooked prime rib roast or similar roast of beef
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 tablespoons flour

Directions

  1. (After roasting a seasoned prime rib beef cut) Pour all of fat and vegetables out of roasting pan. We took the stainless steel roasting pan, and placed it over two burners of our stove top. Add beef stock, red wine, and simmer the liquid over medium heat on both burners – scraping the drippings from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. After we had stirred up as much of the drippings as possible, pour the mixture into a sauce pan Add two bay leaves Let the mixture simmer on low for about 15 minutes. The mixture will gradually thicken. To increase the thickness – add some (1/3 cup or so) of the liquid to a separate bowl – and whisk together with 1 tablespoon of softened butter 2 tablespoons of flour. Add the roux just a little bit at a time – keeping in mind that the gravy will thicken a tad more if left to stand! Whisk this “roux” back into the gravy. Remove the bay leaves and get ready to be “wowed”.
