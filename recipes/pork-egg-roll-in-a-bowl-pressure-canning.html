Pork Egg Roll in a Bowl ~ Pressure Canning

Source: https://i0.wp.com/jenigough.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/08/2A09C2B3-671C-412B-A8E3-11E9E508F85A.jpg?resize=600%2C600&ssl=1

Ingredients

  • 8 lbs Pork shoulder, cut into 1 1/2 inch cubes
  • Salt & pepper
  • 17 cups Chicken or pork broth
  • 1 cup Soy sauce
  • 14 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp Ground ginger or 2 tbsp fresh grated
  • 2 – 4 tbsp Sriracha sauce
  • 1/2 head Cabbage, sliced thin
  • 1 Large onion, chopped
  • 3 Large carrots, grated

Directions

  1. Wash jars in hot, soapy water and prepare pressure canner with water, per canner instructions. Heat oven to 425°. Sprinkle salt and pepper over meat and spread out onto baking sheet pans in a single layer. Roast in oven for 30 minutes, stirring half way in between. Bring pressure canner to a low simmer. Mix cabbage, carrots and onions in a large bowl, set aside until ready to fill jars. Prepare broth by adding broth, soy sauce, garlic and ginger to a large pot. Bring to a boil, cover. lower heat and simmer 10 minutes or until ready to can. Divide hot meat in to jars, add cabbage mixture until jars are 3/4 full, pour in broth leaving 1 inch headspace. De=bubble, adding more both to make 1 inch headspace if needed. Wipe rims, add lids and rings to fingertip tight. Put jars into simmering pressure canner. Process quarts 90 minutes and pints 75 minutes.
