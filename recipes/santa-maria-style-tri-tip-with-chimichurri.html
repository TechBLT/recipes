Santa Maria Style Tri-Tip with Chimichurri

Source: https://disneydishesblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/Hot-Grill-768x511.jpg

Ingredients

  • For the Santa Maria Style Rub
  • ¼
  • cup – Kosher or Sea Salt
  • ¼
  • cup – Brown Sugar
  • 2
  • tbls – Chili Powder
  • 1
  • tbls – Granulated Garlic
  • 1
  • tbls – Granulated Onion
  • ½
  • tsp – Cayenne Pepper
  • 1
  • tsp – Smoked Paprika
  • ½
  • tsp – Black Pepper
  • For the Chimichurri:
  • 2
  • cups – Fresh Italian Parsley – Stems removed
  • 1
  • cup – Fresh Cilantro – Stems removed
  • ½
  • cup – Fresh Oregano – Stems removed
  • 2
  • cloves – Garlic – Minced
  • 1
  • tbls – Red Wine Vinegar
  • ½
  • tsp – Salt
  • 1
  • tbls – Olive Oil
  • ¼
  • tsp – Crushed Red Pepper Flakes

Directions

  1. For the Santa Maria Style Rub
  2. Place the Kosher or Sea Salt, Brown Sugar, Chili Powder, Granulated Garlic, Granulated Onion, Cayenne Pepper, Smoked Paprika and Black Pepper into a small bowl
  3. Stir until thoroughly combined
  4. For the Santa Maria Style Tri-Tip
  5. Place the Tri-Tip Roast into a large bowl or baking dish. Add about ½ cup of the Santa Maria Style Rub and toss to coat. Add more as necessary to make sure the entire roast is coated with the rub
  6. Cover the Tri-Tip with a couple of layers of Plastic Wrap and place in a Baking Dish. Let marinate in the refrigerator for 1 to 6 hours
  7. Once fully marinated, remove from the refrigerator and unwrap. Allow to the roast to sit for 30 – 60
  8. Prepare your grill for direct and indirect cooking
  9. Place the Tri-Tip over the direct heat and sear, uncovered for approximately 10 minutes. Turn over and sear the other side for an additional 10 minutes
  10. Move the seared Tri-Tip to the indirect heat side of the grill, cover and allow to roast for an additional 10 to 20 minutes or until a thermometer inserted in the thickest portion reaches 125ºF
  11. Remove from heat, cover and allow the Tri-Tip to rest for 10 – 15 minutes
  12. For the Chimichurri:
  13. Place the Fresh Italian Parsley, Fresh Cilantro, Fresh Oregano, Garlic, Red Wine Vinegar and Salt into a Food Processor. Pulse a few times to roughly chop the herbs
  14. Lightly drizzle in the Olive Oil while continuing to pulse the Food Processor. Continue until well combined the Herbs are chopped to your desired consistency
  15. Spoon the Chimichurri into a small bowl. Add the Crushed Red Pepper Flakes and stir to combine
  16. Taste and adjust seasonings, as necessary, with Salt, Crushed Red Pepper Flakes and/or Red Wine Vinegar
  17. Reserve until ready to serve
  18. Plating
  19. Slice the Santa Maria Style Tri-Tip thinly, against the grain
  20. Layer the Tri-Tip slices on individual plates or a large serving plate
  21. Spoon the Chimicurri over the top-center of the layered Tri-Tip
  22. Serve immediately
