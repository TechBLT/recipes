Slow Cooker French Onion Ground Beef (7-Quart)

Ingredients

  • 3
  • lb ground beef
  • 5
  • large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 5
  • tbsp butter
  • 5
  • cloves garlic, minced
  • 3
  • tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 1/2
  • tbsp beef bouillon paste or 3 bouillon cubes
  • 3
  • cups beef broth
  • 1 1/2
  • tsp dried thyme
  • 1
  • tsp black pepper
  • 1
  • tsp salt
  • 1/3
  • cup dry white wine or sherry (optional)
  • 1 1/2
  • cups shredded Gruyère or Swiss cheese (for serving)
  • Fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Directions

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat and brown the ground beef thoroughly in batches if needed. Season lightly with salt and pepper. Drain excess fat and transfer beef to the slow cooker.
  2. In the same skillet, melt butter and add sliced onions. Cook 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply caramelized. Add garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Deglaze the skillet with wine or sherry if using, scraping up all browned bits. Transfer onion mixture to the slow cooker.
  4. Add Worcestershire sauce, beef bouillon, beef broth, thyme, remaining salt and pepper. Stir well.
  5. Cover and cook on LOW for 6–8 hours (preferred) or HIGH for 3–4 hours.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve over mashed potatoes, egg noodles, polenta, or toasted bread. Top with shredded Gruyère and fresh parsley if desired.
