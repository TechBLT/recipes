How to Can Garden Tomatoes

Source: https://i0.wp.com/hopewellheightsblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/09/IMG_5947.jpg?resize=600%2C400&ssl=1

Ingredients

  • 5
  • lbs tomatoes
  • 1
  • lb carrots
  • 1
  • onion
  • 8
  • cloves garlic
  • 1/2
  • cup dry red wine
  • 1
  • T sea salt
  • 1/4
  • t black pepper
  • beef bones (optional)
  • 1/2
  • cup fresh herb mixture like basil, parley, and oregano OR 2 T dried herb mixture

Directions

  1. SAUCE INSTRUCTIONS
  2. wash, rinse, and dry all produce to be used
  3. rough chop tomatoes, onions, carrots, and garlic and add them all to a stock pot
  4. add wine, salt & pepper, herbs, and beef bones
  5. simmer on low for a few hours
  6. remove from heat and remove beef bones
  7. puree with a blender, or use an immersion blender to puree right in the pot you cooked in (this is what I do because it is less mess and dishes.
  8. serve right away, freeze, or can
  9. PRESSURE CANNING INSTRUCTIONS
  10. add 2-3 inches of water to the pressure canner
  11. Place clean jars in the pressure canner and heat to 140-180F to pre-heat the jars so they don't bust when the hot sauce is added.
  12. use a funnel and ladle to transfer the sauce into clean, hot jars
  13. remove air bubbles with a silicon utensil
  14. clean and dry the jar rims
  15. screw lids and rings on fingertip tight
  16. place the sealed jars into the pressure canner and secure the lid
  17. turn up the heat and vent the steam for 10 minutes
  18. place the weight/pressure regulator on the vent pipe
  19. process for 75 minutes for pints and 90 minutes for quarts at 10 PSI
  20. remove from heat and allow the canner to depressurize with the pressure regulator in place, this will take a while. Do not "bleed" pressure by removing the regulator/weight.
  21. Once the gauge is at 0, remove the lid and transfer the jars to cool on a towel. Do not place jars directly onto a cold surface, this may cause them to bust.
  22. Once cooled, remove the rings, check the seals, and store them in a cool, dark place like a pantry or basement.
