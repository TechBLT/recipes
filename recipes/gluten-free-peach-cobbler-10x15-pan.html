Gluten-Free Peach Cobbler (10x15 Pan)

Ingredients

  • 9–10 ripe peaches (or 3½–4 lbs frozen, thawed & drained)
  • 3/4 cup Allulose (or sugar)
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp cornstarch
  • Topping:
  • 1 1/3 cups gluten-free all-purpose flour (Bob’s Red Mill 1-to-1)
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/3 tsp baking soda
  • 1/3 tsp salt
  • 4 Tbsp cold butter, cubed
  • 3 Tbsp Allulose (or sugar)
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk (or milk with lemon juice)

Directions

  1. 1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Grease a 10x15 Pyrex pan.
  2. In a large bowl, toss peaches with Allulose, lemon juice, cinnamon, vanilla, and cornstarch. Spread evenly in baking dish.
  3. In another bowl, whisk flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and Allulose. Cut in butter with a fork until crumbly.
  4. Stir in buttermilk until just combined (batter will be thick).
  5. Drop spoonfuls of batter over the fruit. It doesn’t need to cover completely.
  6. Bake 40–45 minutes until topping is golden and fruit is bubbling.
  7. Rest 10 minutes before serving. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream.
