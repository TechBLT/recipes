One-Dish Ground Turkey Sweet Potato Skillet

Source: https://www.tasteofhome.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/One-Dish-Ground-Turkey-Sweet-Potato-Skillet_EXPS_TOHD24_246076_NicolePerry_6.jpg

Ingredients

  • 1
  • pound ground turkey
  • 1
  • medium onion, chopped
  • 1
  • shallot, finely chopped
  • 3
  • garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4
  • cup tomato paste
  • 1
  • medium sweet potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1
  • cup chicken broth
  • 2
  • teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1/2
  • teaspoon salt
  • 1/4
  • teaspoon pepper
  • 3
  • cups chopped fresh kale
  • Dash crushed red pepper flakes
  • 1
  • medium ripe avocado, peeled and sliced
  • Minced fresh mint, optional

Directions

  1. Cook the turkey and vegetables
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground turkey, onion, shallot and garlic over medium heat, stirring frequently and breaking up the meat into crumbles, until the turkey is no longer pink and the vegetables are tender, 8 to 10 minutes. Drain any excess fat from the skillet. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for one minute longer, allowing the flavors to meld.
  3. Simmer the sweet potatoes and kale
  4. Add the peeled and cubed sweet potatoes, chicken broth, paprika, salt and pepper to the skillet, stirring to combine. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat. Simmer, covered, until the sweet potatoes are tender, about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Stir in the chopped kale and crushed red pepper flakes, then cook and stir until the kale is wilted, about two minutes longer. Serve the skillet with sliced avocado and fresh mint, if desired.
  5. In a large skillet, cook turkey, onion, shallot and garlic over medium heat until turkey is no longer pink and vegetables are tender, 8-10 minutes, breaking up turkey into crumbles; drain. Add tomato paste; cook and stir 1 minute longer.
  6. Add sweet potato, broth, smoked paprika, salt and pepper. Bring to a boil; reduce heat. Simmer, covered, until sweet potato is tender, about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add kale and red pepper flakes; cook and stir until kale is wilted, about 2 minutes longer. Serve with avocado and, if desired, mint.
