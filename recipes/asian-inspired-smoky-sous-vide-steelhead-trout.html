Asian-Inspired Smoky Sous Vide Steelhead Trout

Ingredients

  • 2 steelhead trout fillets
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp freshly ground white pepper
  • 1 thin slice fresh ginger
  • 1 garlic clove, smashed
  • 1 green onion, cut into 3–4 inch pieces
  • 1 tsp low-sodium soy sauce (or tamari)
  • 1/2 tsp honey or brown sugar
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1–2 drops liquid smoke or pinch smoked paprika
  • ---Glaze---
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce (or tamari)
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1–2 drops liquid smoke (optional)

Directions

  1. Pat fish dry. Season with salt and white pepper. Place fillets in a sous vide bag with ginger, garlic, green onion, soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, and smoky element. Seal bag. Sous vide at 125°F (52°C) for 30–45 min for medium texture. While fish cooks, make glaze: Combine soy sauce, mirin, honey, rice vinegar, smoked paprika, and liquid smoke in a small saucepan. Simmer until syrupy, 3–4 min. Remove fish from bag, discard aromatics, pat dry. Quick sear in hot skillet with neutral oil, 15–30 sec per side. Brush glaze over fish in skillet, letting it bubble for a few seconds. Serve immediately with rice and vegetables.
