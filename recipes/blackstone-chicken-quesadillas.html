Blackstone Chicken Quesadillas

Source: https://grillonadime.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/grill-chicken-quesadilla-2.jpg

Ingredients

  • Chicken Breast, diced into 1 inch pieces – You can also use chicken thighs.
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Taco Seasoning
  • Salsa
  • Cheddar Cheese, shredded
  • Monterey Jack Cheese, shredded
  • Flour Tortillas, burrito size
  • 1 x 2x 3x
  • 1 lb Chicken Breast diced into 1 inch pieces
  • 1 Tbsp Vegetable Oil
  • 2 Tbsp Taco Seasoning
  • 1/4 cup Salsa
  • 1 1/2 cups Cheddar Cheese shredded
  • 1 1/2 cups Monterey Jack Cheese shredded
  • 4 Flour Tortillas burrito size

Directions

  1. How to Cook Chicken Quesadilla on Blackstone Griddle: Prepare Blackstone Griddle – Preheat the Blackstone Grill over medium high heat about 400 degrees. Spread the vegetable oil onto the grill. Add Chicken to Griddle and Season – Place the chicken onto the grill in an even layer. Season it with the taco seasoning. Cook Chicken – Sauté the chicken for 3-4 minutes per side until it’s browned slightly. Add Salsa – Then top the chicken with salsa and toss the chicken to thoroughly coat the chicken with the salsa. Continue to cook the chicken until it’s cooked through (internal temperature of 165 degrees F) for approximately 3-4 more minutes. Place chicken on the side – Push the chicken to one side of the grill. Reduce the Blackstone temperature to medium. Add tortillas to griddle – Place the tortillas on the flat top griddle. Top each tortillas a small amount of each cheese, then layer the chicken on top of melted cheese and then add more cheese. Fold Tortilla – Carefully fold each the tortillas in half making a half moon shape. You can also add a tortilla on top. Cool until Golden Brown – Cook until one side of the tortilla is golden brown (1-2 minutes). Flip and then cook until the other side is golden brown as well (1-2 minutes). Watch them closely as they do brown quickly. Serve and Enjoy – Remove the quesadillas onto a cutting board or baking sheet. Slice them and they they are ready to serve and enjoy. Variation Ideas: Vegetable Oil – I used vegetable oil in this recipe but any oil with a high smoke point will work great. Avocado oil, coconut oil or canola oil would work great in this recipe. We recommend not using olive oil since it doesn’t have a high smoke point. Change the Cheese – You can easily use your favorite type of shredded cheeses in this quesadilla recipe. Use your favorite. We loved using pepper jack cheese. Change the Seasoning – Garlic salt, onion powder or use what you have. Add in vegetables – Cook the vegetables right on the griddle until tender and chicken is thoroughly. Then add them to your quesadilla. We love spinach, onion, peppers. Place in the Oven – If you prefer or don’t have a blackstone, you can make these on the stove top or in the toaster oven. Dipping Sauces – Add your favorite dipping sauces. We like salsa, sour cream, and guacamole. Preheat the Blackstone Grill over medium high heat (approximately 400 degrees F). Spread the vegetable oil onto the grill. Place the chicken onto the grill in an even layer. Season it with the taco seasoning. Sauté the chicken for 3-4 minutes per side until it’s browned slightly. Then top the chicken with salsa and toss the chicken to thoroughly coat the chicken with the salsa. Continue to cook the chicken until it’s cooked through (internal temperature of 165 degrees F) for approximately 3-4 more minutes. Push the chicken to one side of the grill. Reduce the Blackstone temperature to medium. Place the tortillas on the grill. Top each tortillas a small amount of each cheese, then layer the chicken on top and then add more cheese. Carefully fold each the tortillas in half making a half moon shape. Cook until one side of the tortilla is golden brown (1-2 minutes). Flip and then cook until the other side is golden brown as well (1-2 minutes). Watch them closely as they do brown quickly. Remove the quesadillas onto a cutting board. Slice them and they they are ready to serve and enjoy!
