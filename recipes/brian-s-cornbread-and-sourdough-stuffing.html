Brian's Cornbread and Sourdough Stuffing

Ingredients

  • 32
  • oz Boudin Sourdough Stuffing Mix
  • 24
  • oz Pepperidge Farm Cornbread Stuffing Mix (2 × 12 oz bags)
  • 1.5
  • lbs Jimmy Dean Sage Sausage, browned
  • 1
  • stick (8 tbsp) butter
  • 3
  • cups diced yellow onion
  • 2
  • cups diced celery
  • 2–3
  • tbsp minced garlic
  • 2
  • tbsp dry sherry
  • 1–1.5
  • tsp poultry seasoning
  • 1
  • tsp dried sage
  • 1/2
  • tsp dried thyme
  • Black pepper, to taste
  • 13–14
  • cups turkey stock (6.5–7 cups per pan)
  • Optional: 2–3 tbsp melted butter per pan (for drizzling before baking)
  • Optional: Fresh parsley for garnish

Directions

  1. 1. Brown the 1.5 lbs sage sausage in a skillet until deeply caramelized. Set aside.
  2. 2. In a large pot, melt 1 stick of butter. Add onions and celery and sauté 8–10 minutes until soft.
  3. 3. Add 2 tbsp sherry and cook 1–2 minutes until nearly evaporated.
  4. 4. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  5. 5. Add poultry seasoning, dried sage, dried thyme, and black pepper. Stir and remove from heat.
  6. 6. In a large bowl, combine the Boudin sourdough stuffing mix, Pepperidge Farm cornbread mix, and all seasoning packets.
  7. 7. Add the browned sausage and sautéed aromatics to the dry stuffing.
  8. 8. Warm 13–14 cups of turkey stock. Slowly pour into the stuffing mixture, tossing until evenly moistened. Let rest 5 minutes.
  9. 9. Divide evenly between two buttered 13×9 pans (each holds 14 cups). Drizzle each with 2–3 tbsp melted butter if desired.
  10. 10. Cover and refrigerate until baking day. Safe to hold overnight.
  11. 11. To bake: Cover with foil and bake at 350°F for 30 minutes. Uncover and bake an additional 15–20 minutes to brown.
  12. 12. Serve hot. Perfect for making one pan Thursday and one pan Friday.
