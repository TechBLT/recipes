BANCHERO’S SOUP RECIPE | a BAY AREA THROWBACK

Source: https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/6208c478b35a6d27894183d7/c24acfb0-471d-4679-9b64-ae25e135800f/bancheros_soup.JPG?format=1500w

Ingredients

  • 8
  • cups of Chicken Stock
  • 1
  • Potato (peeled and diced into small cubes)
  • 1-2
  • Carrots, peeled and small dice
  • 1-2
  • Celery Stalks, small dice
  • 3
  • oz Pearl Barley
  • 3
  • oz Egg Pastina Pasta or Orzo (egg pastina preferred)
  • ½
  • teaspoon Garlic Powder
  • 4
  • oz Tomato Paste
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Parmesan Cheese for garnish when serving
  • Bonus Recipe for Garlic Bread
  • 2
  • tbsp Olive Oil
  • ½
  • Stick Salted Butter
  • 4
  • cloves of garlic finely minced
  • 1
  • loaf of bread cut into serving pieces

Directions

  1. Bring Chicken Stock to a boil. Chop celery, carrots and potato into cube size pieces.
  2. Add barley and garlic powder and let everything boil for 5 mins.
  3. Add celery, carrots, potato let boil for 5 mins.
  4. Lower heat to medium add tomato sauce and stir in. Let the soup cook on medium for about 20 mins.
  5. Add pasta. Let the soup cook for about 10 mins or until done. If pasta is done cooking but veggies are not tender, then lower heat and simmer until done.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve with a sprinkle of parmesan to garnish.
  8. The assembly of the soup was pretty easy to follow. The prepping and cutting of the vegetables probably took the most time. After making this a couple of times, it gets easier to put together.
