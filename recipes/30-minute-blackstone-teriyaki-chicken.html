30-Minute Blackstone Teriyaki Chicken

Source: https://wendypolisi.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/02/blackstone-teriyaki-chicken-h-150x225.jpg

Ingredients

  • Teriyaki Sauce
  • ⅓
  • cup mirin rice wine
  • ⅓
  • cup soy sauce
  • ¼
  • cup water or chicken broth
  • 2 ½
  • tablespoons sugar
  • 2
  • teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1 ½
  • teaspoons fresh ginger
  • ⅛
  • teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
  • Chicken and Vegetables
  • 2
  • pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs cut into strips
  • 1
  • teaspoon sea salt
  • ½
  • teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
  • Avocado oil for the griddle
  • 1
  • red bell pepper seeded and cut into strips
  • 8
  • ounces snap peas trimmed
  • For Finishing
  • 1
  • tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1
  • tablespoon sesame seeds

Directions

  1. First, make the teriyaki sauce by combining the mirin, soy sauce, water or chicken broth, sugar, cornstarch, ginger, garlic and red pepper flakes in a small saucepan over medium low heat.
  2. Cook until thickened.
  3. You can do this ahead of time; make sure if you refrigerate the sauce to let it come back to room temperature before proceeding with making Blackstone chicken teriyaki.
  4. Next, season the chicken with salt and pepper. I like to do this an hour ahead (or up to a day) if I have time.
  5. Preheat your griddle to medium heat and oil it well. Resist the temptation to crank up the heat on your flat top grill too high because the sugar in the sauce will burn easily.
  6. Add the chicken and veggies and drizzle with 3 tablespoons of the sauce. Cook for 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  7. Drizzle with sesame oil and stir well.
  8. Transfer to a bowl or plate. Serve with remaining teriyaki sauce and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Enjoy!
  9. Tips & Tricks
  10. Avoid the temptation to use medium-high heat. The sugars in the sauce will burn if your griddle is too hot. (And this can happen on a Blackstone much quicker than a stovetop.)
  11. If you don't have mirin, you can substitute rice wine vinegar or apple cider vinegar mixed with a tablespoon of sugar.
  12. If your teriyaki sauce is too thin, add a little more cornstarch to thicken it up.
  13. For extra flavor and crunch, consider topping your teriyaki bowls with toasted sesame seeds, green onions, and cashews.
  14. Serving Suggestions
  15. I like teriyaki chicken thighs on the Blackstone griddle served with rice. Use my Instant Pot jasmine rice or slow cooker rice recipe for fool-proof rice every time. Blackstone fried rice is also fabulous with this easy recipe.
  16. You could also serve this over rice noodles or soba noodles. For a different twist, try using brown rice or quinoa instead of white rice.
  17. Combine the mirin, soy sauce, water, sugar, cornstarch, ginger, and crushed red pepper flakes in a small saucepan over medium-low heat. Cook until thickened, about 8 minutes.
  18. Season the chicken with salt and pepper.
  19. Preheat your griddle to medium heat and oil it well.
  20. Add the chicken and veggies and drizzle with 3 tablespoons of the sauce.
  21. Cook for 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  22. Drizzle with the sesame oil and stir well. Remove from heat. Serve with the remaining sauce.
