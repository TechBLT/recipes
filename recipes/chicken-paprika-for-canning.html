Chicken Paprika for Canning

Source: https://i0.wp.com/jenigough.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/Chicken-Paprika-2.jpg?resize=512%2C530&ssl=1

Ingredients

  • 10 1/2
  • lbs chicken, cut into 1 1/2 inch chunks
  • 2
  • onions, chopped
  • 2
  • red bell peppers, chopped
  • 1
  • 28 oz can diced tomatoes
  • 12
  • cups chicken broth
  • 14
  • cloves garlic, minced
  • 1
  • tbsp onion powder
  • 2
  • tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2
  • tsp kosher, sea or canning salt
  • 8
  • tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika

Directions

  1. Inspect jars for cracks and chips. Wash jars, lids and rings in hot, soapy water. Prepare pressure canner per canner instructions, filling with water. Bring canner to a low simmer.
  2. Let’s make the broth: Pour chicken broth and tomatoes into a large pot and add garlic and seasonings. Bring to a boil, cover, lower heat and simmer 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, let’s fill the jars. Quarts – To each jar add: 1 1/2 pounds of chicken. Divide onions and bell peppers evenly among jars. Fill jars with broth leaving 1-inch headspace. Remove air bubbles and add extra broth if needed to bring level back up to 1-inch headspace. Wipe rims and place on lids and rings fingertip tight. Add jars directly to simmering canner. Pints – To each jar add: 3/4 pounds of chicken. Divide onions and bell peppers evenly among jars. Fill jars with broth leaving 1-inch headspace. Remove air bubbles and add extra broth if needed to bring level back up to 1-inch headspace. Wipe rims and place on lids and rings fingertip tight. Add jars directly to simmering canner.
  4. *If preferred, mix all ingredients into a large bowl and divide mixture evenly among jars.
  5. Process quarts for 90 minutes and pints for 75 minutes.
