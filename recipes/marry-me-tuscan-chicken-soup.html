Marry Me Tuscan Chicken Soup

Source: https://smokinandgrillinwitab.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Marry-me-Chicken-Soup-3-276x276.jpg

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 1–1½ pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs (cut into 1-inch pieces)
  • 1 tablespoon AB’s A Rub
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning (divided)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • ½ cup diced carrots
  • ½ cup diced celery
  • ½ cup diced onions
  • ¼ cup diced sun-dried tomatoes
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste (optional, adds richness and color)
  • 6–8 cups chicken broth (start with 6; add more if you prefer a thinner soup)
  • 6 oz small pasta (Italian shells, ditalini, or elbows)
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • ½–1 cup freshly grated Parmesan Reggiano cheese
  • 2½–3 cups fresh spinach

Directions

  1. 1. BROWN THE CHICKEN Heat olive oil in a large soup pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Season the diced chicken withAB’s A Rub, ½ teaspoon Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken to the pot and cook for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. SAUTÉ THE VEGETABLES In the same pot, add onions, carrots, celery, sun-dried tomatoes, and garlic. Cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring often, until the vegetables soften and release their aroma.
  3. ADD FLOUR AND TOMATO PASTE Sprinkle the flour evenly over the vegetables, stirring continuously to coat. Cook for 1 minute to eliminate the raw flour taste. If using, stir in tomato paste to enhance richness.
  4. ADD BROTH AND SIMMER Gradually whisk in the chicken broth while scraping the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. Return the chicken to the pot and bring to a gentle boil.
  5. ADD PASTA AND SEASONING Stir in the pasta, the remaining ½ teaspoon of Italian seasoning, and another light sprinkle of AB’s A Rub. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 20 minutes, until the pasta is tender and the chicken is fully cooked. (If you plan to store leftovers, cook the pasta separately and add it to each serving to prevent it from soaking up too much broth.)
  6. FINISH THE SOUP Add the heavy cream, spinach, and Parmesan cheese. Simmer for 5 minutes until the spinach wilts and the soup thickens slightly.
  7. TASTE AND ADJUST Taste the soup and adjust seasoning with more salt, pepper, or AB’s A Rub as desired. Serve immediately.
  8. Spicy Tuscan Version Add a pinch of crushed red pepper flakes or a dash of AB’s Hot Rub to kick up the heat.
  9. Dairy-Free Option Replace the heavy cream with unsweetened coconut milk or a plant-based alternative for a lighter version that’s still creamy.
  10. Low-Carb Version Skip the pasta and add extra spinach, zucchini noodles, or riced cauliflower for a keto-friendly twist.
  11. Creamy Sausage & Chicken Tuscan Soup Mix in half a pound of cooked Italian sausage with the chicken for added heartiness and extra flavor depth.
  12. Vegetarian Adaptation Use vegetable broth, omit the chicken, and substitute with white beans or chickpeas for a plant-based variation. SERVING SUGGESTIONS With Bread: Serve with crusty garlic bread, focaccia, or a toasted baguette for dipping. Salad Pairing: A crisp Caesar or Italian chopped salad balances the richness perfectly. Make it a Meal: Add a side of roasted vegetables or a simple antipasto platter. Garnish: Top each bowl with shaved Parmesan, a drizzle of olive oil, or a sprinkle of fresh basil for restaurant-style presentation. STORAGE & REHEATING TIPS Refrigerate: Store in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Freeze: Leave out the pasta and spinach, then freeze the base for up to 3 months. Add those ingredients fresh when reheating. Reheat: Warm gently on the stovetop over medium heat, adding a splash of broth or cream to restore texture. Can I use rotisserie chicken instead of raw chicken? Yes. Add shredded rotisserie chicken in Step 4 after adding the broth. It saves time and adds a deeper roasted flavor. Can I use a different pasta? Absolutely. Any small pasta such as orzo, rotini, or penne works well. Just adjust the cooking time so the pasta stays al dente. What if my soup gets too thick? Add more chicken broth or a splash of cream until you reach your desired consistency. Can I make this in a slow cooker? Yes. Add all ingredients except the cream, spinach, and pasta to your slow cooker and cook on low for 6 hours. Stir in cream, spinach, and cooked pasta before serving. While the oil warms, season the diced chicken generously with AB’s A Rub, half of the Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Once the pot is hot, add the chicken and cook for about four to five minutes, stirring occasionally, until it’s golden brown on all sides. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside for later. In the same pot, add the onions, carrots, celery, sun-dried tomatoes, and garlic. Sauté the vegetables for three to four minutes, stirring often, until they begin to soften and release their aroma. The goal here is to build a flavorful base — those browned bits on the bottom of the pot will help deepen the soup’s flavor. Next, sprinkle the flour evenly over the vegetables and stir until everything is coated. Let it cook for about a minute to remove the raw flour taste, then stir in the tomato paste if you’re using it. This adds a touch of richness and gives the soup a beautiful color. Slowly pour in the chicken broth, whisking as you go to prevent lumps and to deglaze the pot. Use a wooden spoon to scrape up any browned bits left from the chicken — that’s pure flavor. Once the broth is smooth and bubbling, return the chicken to the pot and bring it all to a gentle boil. When the broth starts to simmer, stir in the pasta, the remaining Italian seasoning, and another small pinch of AB’s A Rub. Lower the heat, cover the pot, and let the soup simmer for about twenty minutes, or until the pasta is tender and the chicken is fully cooked through. If you plan to store leftovers, consider cooking the pasta separately and stirring it in before serving so it doesn’t absorb too much broth. To finish, stir in the heavy cream, fresh spinach, and Parmesan cheese. Allow the soup to simmer uncovered for another five minutes, just until the spinach wilts and the broth thickens into a rich, creamy texture. Taste the soup before serving, adjusting with extra salt, pepper, or AB’s A Rub if needed. Serve immediately while hot, topped with a sprinkle of Parmesan and a drizzle of olive oil for extra richness.
