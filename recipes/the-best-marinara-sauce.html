The Best Marinara Sauce

Source: https://www.goodlifeeats.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/Best-Ever-Homemade-Classic-Marinara-Sauce-photo-40x60.jpg

Ingredients

  • 1/2
  • cup Extra-Virgin Olive Oil
  • 4 1/2
  • tablespoons minced Fresh Garlic Cloves
  • 2 - 4
  • pinches Crushed Red Pepper Flakes
  • 2 1/4
  • teaspoon dried Oregano
  • 6
  • 28-ounce cans Crushed Tomatoes (do not drain)
  • 2
  • teaspoons Sea Salt
  • 2
  • teaspoons Granulated Sugar
  • 1 1/2
  • cup Chianti or Sangiovese Wine

Directions

  1. SAUTÉ THE AROMATICS
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot (at least 7 quarts) over medium heat.
  3. Add the garlic, crushed red pepper, and oregano.
  4. Sauté for 2–3 minutes, stirring frequently, until the garlic is fragrant but not browned.
  5. ADD THE TOMATOES & SEASONINGS
  6. Slowly pour in the crushed tomatoes, one can at a time, stirring after each addition to fully incorporate.
  7. Once all tomatoes have been added, stir in the salt, sugar, and red wine.
  8. Use a wire whisk to mix everything well.
  9. SIMMER LOW & SLOW
  10. Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer over medium heat.
  11. Reduce the heat to low and let it cook uncovered for at least 6 hours, ideally 8 hours.
  12. Stir every hour with a wooden spoon, scraping the bottom of the pot to prevent burning.
  13. FINAL ADJUSTMENTS & SERVING
  14. Taste the sauce before serving. If needed, add up to 1 more teaspoon of salt, depending on how much it has reduced.
  15. Use immediately or let cool completely before storing. This sauce freezes well for future meals!
  16. HOW TO FREEZE MARINARA SAUCE
  17. Let the sauce cool completely, then transfer to freezer-safe containers or zip-top bags (quart or gallon size).
  18. For bags – Seal tightly, double bag for extra protection, and lay flat to freeze. Label with contents and date.
  19. Portion Sizes – A quart-sized bag holds ~4 cups, and a gallon-sized bag holds ~16 cups. Freeze in portions that fit your family’s needs.
