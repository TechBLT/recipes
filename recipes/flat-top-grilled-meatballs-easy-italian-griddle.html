Flat Top Grilled Meatballs – Easy Italian Griddle

Source: https://theflattopking.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/flat-top-grilled-meatballs-recipe-2.jpg

Ingredients

  • 1
  • lb. ground beef
  • 1
  • lb. ground sausage
  • 1/2
  • lb. ground mild Italian sausage
  • 2
  • slices white bread dried on counter overnight
  • 2
  • large eggs
  • 1/2
  • white onion grated
  • 3/4
  • cup ricotta
  • 1/2
  • cup grated pecorino romano cheese or substitute parmesan
  • 5
  • fresh basil leaves chopped
  • 1/4
  • cup fresh parsley chopped
  • 1
  • head roasted garlic
  • salt and pepper

Directions

  1. Tear 2 slices of bread into small pieces and add to a large bowl.
  2. Add eggs, the ground meats, grated onion, ricotta and pecorino ramono cheeses, basil, parsley, roasted garlic cloves, and salt and pepper to the bowl.
  3. Use your hands to combine all of the meatball ingredients thoroughly.
  4. Roll the meatball mixture into balls slightly smaller than a golf ball and place on a plate or baking sheet. The mixture should yield about 40 meatballs.
  5. Refrigerate meatballs for at least one hour before cooking on the griddle.
  6. Preheat flat top grill or griddle on low.
  7. Add chilled meatballs to the griddle and allow to cook on the first side until they develop a nice crust and start to release from the griddle surface.
  8. Carefully flip the meatballs to the other side, being sure to "scrape" the crust up from the griddle surface.
  9. Allow to cook on the second side until a nice crust develops.
  10. Continue to flip the meatballs while they finish cooking so the crust develops evenly and they don't burn. Cook until an internal thermometer reads 160 degrees.
  11. Remove cooked meatballs from the griddle and transfer to a plate or wire cooling rack. Allow the meatballs to cool before serving.
