Green Enchilada Sauce for Pressure Canning

Source: https://i0.wp.com/jenigough.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/2AD5A5F6-C571-455C-B9F2-AF198E4A3100-530x530.jpg?resize=530%2C530&ssl=1

Ingredients

  • 3
  • qts Roasted green chilis, peeled, seeded and chopped (about 12 lbs)
  • 2
  • tbsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • 1
  • Extra large white onion, chopped
  • 14
  • Cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 1/2
  • qts Chicken broth or water
  • 1
  • tbsp Onion powder
  • 1
  • tbsp Garlic powder
  • 1
  • tbsp Ground cumin
  • 2
  • tbsp Mexican oregano
  • 1
  • tbsp Kosher salt or salt to taste

Directions

  1. Wash jars, lids and rings in hot, soapy water. Prepare pressure canner and fill with water per canner instructions.
  2. Sauté onions in 2 tbsp olive oil for 5 minutes on medium heat. Add garlic and spices, cook for another 3 minutes. Add in chilis and 2 qts of liquid. Cover and cook on medium heat 10 minutes.
  3. Blend hot chilis and the leftover 2 1/2 qts of liquid in batches. Do not overfill and use caution with hot contents.
  4. Pour purée back into pan. Bring to a boil on medium – high heat, cover, turn heat to low and simmer 10 minutes.
  5. Fill jars leaving 1 inch headspace, wipe rims, ad lids and rings to fingertip tight. Place jars into simmering canner.
  6. Process quarts 75 minutes and pints 65 minutes.
